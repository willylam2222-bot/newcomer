Getting around Singapore: the newcomer's transport guide

You don't need a car in Singapore, and most residents don't have one. The MRT plus your contactless card covers almost everything.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 5 min read

Singapore has one of the best public transport systems in the world: a clean, efficient MRT (metro) network backed by extensive buses, all cheap and easy to use. Here's the fastest way to get moving as a newcomer.

Day one: just tap and go (SimplyGo)

You don't need to buy a special transit card. SimplyGo lets you tap most contactless bank cards or your phone (Apple Pay / Google Pay) straight onto MRT and bus readers — fares are charged to your card. That's the simplest option the moment you land. If you prefer, you can still buy a physical stored-value card, but for most newcomers, tapping a card or phone is all you need.

The MRT

  • Fast, air-conditioned, and covers most of the island; trains run roughly early morning until around midnight.
  • Fares are distance-based and cheap — most trips cost a couple of dollars.
  • No eating or drinking anywhere in the system — including water. It's an offence and can be fined.
  • Stand on the correct side of escalators, let passengers off before boarding, and move to the middle of the carriage.

Buses

Buses reach everywhere the MRT doesn't and are just as cheap. Tap your card/phone when you board and when you alight (tap-out) so you're charged the correct distance fare. Google Maps and Citymapper both give accurate live routing.

Grab & taxis

Grab is the dominant ride-hailing app (think local Uber) and also does food delivery and payments. Street taxis are metered and reliable too. Rides are reasonable by global standards but far pricier than the MRT — fine for late nights, luggage or convenience.

Do you need a car? No.

Car ownership is deliberately expensive — you must buy a Certificate of Entitlement (COE) that can cost more than the car, on top of taxes. Public transport plus the occasional Grab is cheaper and usually faster. Most residents never own a car.

Apps to install

  • Google Maps / Citymapper — live transit routing.
  • Grab — rides, food, pay.
  • Your bank's app — to track SimplyGo charges.

Bottom line

Set up SimplyGo (or just tap your phone), download a maps app and Grab, and you can get anywhere in Singapore from day one — cheaply, without a car, and without buying anything special.
