Singapore · Money

Do you actually need cash in Singapore?

Malls, transport and taxis: fully cashless. The hawker stall making your S$4 lunch: maybe not. Here's the real split.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 4 min read

Big guides say "Singapore is cashless" and stop there. The truth has one big exception that affects you daily: food.

Where cards always work

  • Malls, supermarkets, chain cafés and restaurants — contactless everything, Apple/Google Pay included.
  • MRT and buses — tap a contactless bank card or phone directly (SimplyGo).
  • Grab and taxis — in-app or card.

Where they often don't: hawker centres

Many hawker stalls take cash or PayNow QR only — no cards. PayNow needs a local bank account, which tourists don't have. So:

  • Tourists: carry some cash — S$50–100 in small notes covers days of hawker meals and kopi. Withdraw from any ATM; they're everywhere.
  • New residents: your first week is a cash week. Once your bank account and PayNow are live, you can go weeks without touching notes.
The S$2 rule

If a stall's dishes cost a few dollars, assume cash/PayNow first and be pleasantly surprised otherwise. If the shop has aircon and a logo, assume cards work.

Practical notes

  • ATMs from the big banks don't charge local withdrawal fees on their own cards; your home bank's FX fees are the thing to watch — a Wise/Revolut-style card usually beats them.
  • No tipping anywhere, so cash isn't needed for that (see our tipping guide).
  • Kopitiams (coffee shops) and wet markets are the same story as hawkers: small cash is king.
