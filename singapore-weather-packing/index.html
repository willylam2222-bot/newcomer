Singapore weather: hot, wet, and completely predictable

There is one season: hot and humid, with rain. The packing mistake isn't bringing too little — it's bringing the wrong things for a city that lives at 18°C indoors and 32°C outdoors.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 4 min read

Singapore sits almost on the equator. Every month: highs around 31–33°C, humidity 80%+, and a serious thunderstorm never more than a few days away. The northeast monsoon (roughly November–January) brings the wettest stretch; the rest of the year just brings rain less predictably. There is no cold, no dry season worth the name, and no need to time your visit around weather.

The aircon paradox

The genuine climate shock is indoors: malls, offices, cinemas and buses are chilled hard. Locals carry a layer not for weather but for buildings. If you work in an office here, a light cardigan or hoodie at your desk isn't optional — it's survival.

What to actually pack

  • Light, breathable everything — cotton/linen tees, shorts, breathable dresses. You will sweat on any walk; fabric choice matters more than quantity.
  • One light layer — for aircon, not outdoors.
  • Compact umbrella — the single most-used item you'll own. Rain is intense but usually brief; locals wait it out under cover or walk on with a brolly.
  • Sandals + one pair of real shoes — smart-casual covers almost every restaurant; suits are rare outside formal business.
  • Sunscreen — equatorial UV is fierce even on cloudy days.
What NOT to bother packing

Rain jackets (too hot to wear), heavy jeans, more than one warm layer, and a big umbrella. Everything is buyable here cheaply if you miscalculate — Uniqlo, Decathlon and Daiso have you covered.

Living with it

  • Plan outdoor activities for morning; afternoons build heat and storms.
  • The covered-walkway network is extensive — you can often cross whole neighbourhoods dry.
  • Hydrate like it's a sport. Kopi and teh are cultural; water is tactical.
