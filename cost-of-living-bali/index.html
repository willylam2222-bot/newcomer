What Bali actually costs — after the Instagram filter

Bali can be Southeast Asia's best value or a Western-priced lifestyle resort — often on the same street. The variable is which economy you shop in.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 5 min read

Bali runs two parallel economies: the local one (warungs, kosts, markets) priced like Indonesia, and the expat one (villas, brunch cafés, gyms with ring lights) priced like Australia-lite. Your budget is determined almost entirely by the mix you choose.

Rent (monthly, indicative)

SetupCanggu/BerawaUbudUluwatu/Bukit
Kost / guesthouse room~3–6M IDR~2.5–5M~3–5M
Studio / 1BR villa apartment~8–16M IDR~6–12M~7–14M
Private 1–2BR villa with pool~15–30M+ IDR~10–22M~12–25M
  • Monthly beats yearly for flexibility, but yearly contracts cut 20–40% — Bali landlords price commitment steeply.
  • Canggu is the priciest and most crowded; Ubud trades beach for value and calm; Uluwatu sits between. Peak season (Jul–Aug, Dec–Jan) squeezes everything.
  • Check what "including" means: electricity (villas with pools + aircon can add 1.5–3M IDR), pool cleaning, wifi speed in writing.

Food: the two-economy split in one meal

  • Warung lane: nasi campur at 25–50k IDR (~US$2–3) — eating local keeps food under US$250/month, deliciously.
  • Café lane: the famous smoothie-bowl-and-flat-white breakfast runs 80–150k; Western dinners 150–300k. All-café living triples the food line to Western-adjacent numbers.

The fixed overheads people forget

  • Scooter: ~1–1.5M IDR/month rental + fuel (pennies). (Licence + helmet: see our Bali safety guide — this is also your insurance validity.)
  • Visa overhead: VOA + extension ≈ US$65 per 60 days, plus agent fees if used; the E33G KITAS runs real money annually via agents. Budget visas as a utility, not a surprise (see our Bali visa guide).
  • Health insurance: non-optional — Bali's international clinics are good and expensive. US$60–150/month by age and cover.
  • Gyms/coworking: Bali-famous and Bali-priced — 700k–1.5M IDR each per month.
Honest monthly budgets (single person)

~US$900: kost room, warung food, Ubud pace — comfortable local-lane living. ~US$1,500–2,200: villa studio in Canggu, mixed diet, coworking, weekly massage — the standard nomad reality. ~US$3,000+: private pool villa and full café economy — pleasant, and no longer meaningfully cheaper than Lisbon.
