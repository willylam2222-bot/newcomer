Getting into Bali — and staying: what's real in 2026

Bali's visa scene is famously agent-fogged: half the 'rules' online are sales pitches. Here's the actual current menu, from short holiday to legal nomad year.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 5 min read

Indonesia modernised entry rules considerably — e-VOA online, a real remote-worker visa since 2024 — while Bali added its own tourist levy. Because agents monetise confusion here more than anywhere in the region, precision matters:

Short stays

  • Visa on Arrival (VOA): ~IDR 500,000 (~US$32), 30 days, extendable once for another 30 — 60 days max. Buy it as an e-VOA online (molina.imigrasi.go.id / official channels) before flying to skip the airport queue. Available to most nationalities.
  • The Bali tourist levy: a separate IDR 150,000 (~US$10) provincial fee (introduced 2024), paid online (Love Bali portal) or on arrival. It's per entry to Bali, not a visa — pay it, keep the receipt; checks are sporadic but real.
  • B211A visit visa: the longer-stay workhorse — ~60 days extendable to ~180 total, arranged before arrival (this is the one agents sell aggressively; using an agent is normal, just compare prices).

The real nomad option: E33G remote-worker KITAS

Since 2024, Indonesia offers a genuine remote-worker KITAS (E33G): a 1-year residence permit for employees/freelancers of foreign companies, income earned abroad — the headline requirement being ~US$60,000/year foreign income (plus proof of employment and funds). It legalised the Canggu lifestyle for those who qualify, and unlike chained visit visas it's actual residence: re-entry rights, local bank accounts, no 60-day clock.

What to stop believing

  • "Just do visa runs forever" — Indonesia tracks entries; serial VOA chains draw questions, and the crackdowns on 'working on tourist visas' (including influencer deportations) are periodic and real. Working for Indonesian clients/companies on any visit visa is illegal, full stop.
  • "Agents are mandatory" — for VOA/extensions they're optional convenience; for B211A and KITAS they're practical but shop around, prices vary wildly for identical paperwork.
  • Overstay maths: IDR 1,000,000 (~US$65) per day, payable at exit — past a threshold it becomes detention/deportation territory. Calendar your dates; Bali brain is real.
The honest decision tree

Holiday ≤60 days: e-VOA + levy, done. Scouting trip 2–6 months: B211A. Actually living here on foreign income: qualify for the E33G and be legal, or accept that the grey-zone lifestyle now carries real enforcement risk. Working locally: employer-sponsored KITAS, nothing less.
