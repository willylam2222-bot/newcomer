The one rule

Nobody pays you for "using AI." They pay you to solve a problem faster and better than they could themselves. AI is just the tool that lets a beginner deliver at a level people will pay for — today, not in a year.

So the whole game is: pick a service people already buy → use AI to do it 3–5× faster → get it in front of people who have the problem. The 10 prompts in the PDF walk that exact loop. Here are three to start with right now:

1 · Turn your skills into a service people pay for

Most beginners freeze on "what do I even sell?" This turns whatever you're okay at into a concrete paid offer.

Copy · paste · fill the blanksAct as a pragmatic freelance coach. Here's what I can do: [list 3–5 things you're decent at]. My spare time per week: [hours]. Give me 5 specific services I could sell within 7 days, each as: (a) a one-line offer, (b) who buys it, (c) a realistic first price, (d) how AI speeds up delivery. Rank by fastest-to-first-dollar. No vague ideas — only things a beginner can actually deliver this week.

4 · The cold pitch that actually gets replies

Short, specific, about them — not the desperate tone.

Copy · paste · fill the blanksWrite a 5-sentence cold message to a potential client. My service: [offer]. Them: [what I noticed about their business]. Rules: lead with a specific observation about THEM, name one concrete result I can deliver, no flattery, one clear next step. Give me 3 versions in different tones (direct / friendly / expert).

7 · Turn a blank page into a first draft in one shot

The fastest way to look experienced: never start from zero.

Copy · paste · fill the blanksProduce a strong first draft of: [the exact deliverable]. Context: [client, audience, goal, details]. Match this style: [paste a sample they like]. Flag anywhere you're guessing so I can fix it. Then list 3 things I should double-check before sending.
