Malaysia · Money

What Malaysia actually costs — Southeast Asia's quiet value king

Malaysia's pitch is unglamorous and true: first-world infrastructure at Southeast Asian prices, minus Thailand's hype premium. The numbers are quietly the region's best.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 5 min read

Kuala Lumpur consistently ranks among the world's cheapest developed-infrastructure cities for expats — modern condos, good hospitals, real highways, English widely spoken — at prices below Bangkok for equivalent quality. Here's the honest ledger.

Translation: a KL condo with infinity pool and gym runs ~US$450–700/month. Standard deal: 2 months' deposit + half-month utility deposit + 1 month advance; agents are typically paid by landlords. Supply is abundant — KL overbuilt condos for a decade and tenants enjoy the result.

Food: the mamak equilibrium

  • Local lane: hawker/food-court meals RM8–15 (~US$2–3), mamak (24-hour Indian-Muslim joints) roti + teh tarik under RM10, food courts in every mall. Eating brilliantly on RM900–1,200/month is normal.
  • Western lane: café brunch RM25–45, imported groceries at a premium, and alcohol is the big exception — heavily taxed, a bar beer runs RM15–30, so drinkers pay a real lifestyle tax here.

The rest

  • Transport: Grab is cheap and default; KL's rail network (LRT/MRT) is decent and fares are low; petrol is subsidised-cheap and cars are how suburban Malaysia lives.
  • Utilities: electricity with daily aircon ~RM150–350; fibre internet ~RM100–130 for genuinely fast lines; mobile ~RM30–50.
  • Healthcare: the regional sleeper — private hospitals (Gleneagles, Pantai etc.) at a fraction of Singapore prices, so much so that medical tourism is an industry. Expat insurance from ~RM200–500/month by age.
Honest monthly budgets (single person)

~RM4,000 (~US$850): modern condo, local food lane, Grab life — comfortable middle-class KL. ~RM6,500–8,000: nicer condo, mixed diet, weekend trips — the typical expat-professional reality. Families: add schooling — international schools run RM25k–90k/year and are the budget's real swing item, same as everywhere in the region.
