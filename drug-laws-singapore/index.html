Singapore's drug laws, explained soberly

This is the one Singapore rule where 'roughly right' isn't good enough. The law is absolute, the penalties are extreme, and it applies to you.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 5 min read

Most Singapore rules cost you a fine. Drug offences are a different universe: this is one of the strictest drug-law regimes on earth, enforced without exception for foreigners. No listicle tone here — just what you need to understand.

The essentials

  • Zero tolerance. Possession, consumption, import and trafficking of controlled drugs are all serious criminal offences. There is no "personal use" leniency, and cannabis is fully illegal regardless of its status in your home country.
  • Trafficking can carry the death penalty. Above statutory weight thresholds (for example, set amounts of heroin, cocaine, meth or cannabis), trafficking charges can be capital. Singapore has carried out executions for drug offences in recent years — this is not a theoretical law.
  • Presumption clauses. Carrying above certain quantities creates a legal presumption of trafficking — the burden shifts to you. "It wasn't mine" or "I was just carrying a bag for someone" is the exact scenario the law is built around.
  • Consumption can be prosecuted even if it happened abroad for citizens and permanent residents, and travellers can be tested on entry in enforcement operations. The safe assumption for any traveller: arrive clean.
The three absolute rules

1) Never carry a bag, parcel or "gift" through customs for anyone. 2) If you use cannabis legally at home, do not bring any — not edibles, not vape carts, not CBD products (many are controlled here too). 3) Arrive with nothing in your system if you can help it.

Prescription medication is a separate issue

Some legitimate medicines (strong painkillers, sleeping pills, ADHD medication) are controlled substances in Singapore and need advance approval to bring in — see our medication guide. Sorting this before you fly turns a potential criminal issue into paperwork.

Why we wrote this page so plainly

Because the internet is full of jokey "10 crazy Singapore laws" listicles that put chewing gum and capital drug offences in the same breath. They are not the same. Everything else on this site is practical tips; this page is the one warning to take literally and completely.
