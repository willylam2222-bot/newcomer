Changi to the city: the honest comparison

S$2 and 40 minutes, or S$30 and 20 minutes — both are right answers depending on your luggage, your landing time and your patience.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 4 min read

Changi is famously connected. Your three real options:

OptionRough costTime to townWhen it wins
MRT~S$2~35–50 minDaytime arrival, light luggage, going near a station
Grab~S$20–40 (surge varies)~20–30 minGroups, luggage, doorstep delivery
Taxi~S$25–45 incl. airport surcharge~20–30 minSame as Grab; queue at the rank, no app needed

Taking the MRT

Follow signs to the station beneath Terminals 2/3, tap in with any contactless bank card or your phone (no ticket needed — see our SimplyGo guide), and ride. You'll change trains at Tanah Merah or Expo depending on direction — it's a cross-platform hop, easy even with a suitcase.

The late-night catch

Trains stop around 11pm–midnight. Land on a late flight and the MRT is likely not an option by the time you clear immigration — budget for a Grab/taxi instead, and expect mild late-night surge pricing.

Taxis vs Grab

Prices are usually similar. Taxis add an airport surcharge (a few dollars, higher at peak); Grab bakes everything into the quoted fare — what you see is what you pay. If the Grab quote spikes on a rainy Friday night, the taxi rank is often the better deal.

Bottom line

Daytime + one suitcase: take the MRT, it's excellent and nearly free. Family, luggage mountain, or anything after 11pm: just take the car.
