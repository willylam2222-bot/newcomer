What Vietnam actually costs — the region's price floor

Vietnam is where the region's cost curve bottoms out with city life still intact: real coffee culture, fibre internet, $1.50 meals — and a few costs that surprise people.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 5 min read

Among Southeast Asia's expat hubs, Vietnam sits at the value extreme: costs meaningfully below Thailand and Malaysia at similar comfort, with the trade-offs mostly in bureaucracy and infrastructure rather than daily life. The ledger:

Rent (monthly, indicative)

SetupHCMCHanoiDa Nang
Studio/1BR serviced apt~8–15M ₫ (US$320–600)~7–13M ₫~5–10M ₫

  • Serviced apartments (cleaning, laundry, utilities partly bundled) are the expat default and lower friction than bare leases.
  • Deposits run 1–2 months; contracts are flexible by regional standards; landlords handle the required police registration of foreign tenants — confirm yours does.
  • Da Nang is the value-plus-beach play and the nomad favourite for a reason.

Food: the strongest argument

  • Local lane: pho/banh mi/com tam at 30–60k ₫ (US$1.20–2.50); the world's best coffee culture at 25–60k ₫ a cup. Eating superbly on US$150–250/month is real.
  • Western lane: exists in every district at 3–5× local prices, still cheap by Western standards. Imported groceries and wine carry the usual premium.

The rest — and the surprises

  • Transport: Grab bikes cost pennies; a monthly motorbike rental ~1.2–1.8M ₫. (Read our upcoming safety guide before renting — traffic is Vietnam's real hazard.)
  • Internet: fibre ~200–300k ₫ for fast lines; mobile data among the world's cheapest.
  • The surprises: aircon-heavy electricity bills (800k–2M ₫ in hot months), decent health insurance being non-optional (good private hospitals exist in big cities; costs are low but standards vary — insure for evacuation-grade cover), and imported anything (electronics, wine, cheese) costing more than at home.
Honest monthly budgets (single person)

~US$800: Da Nang, local-lane living, serviced studio — comfortable and beachside. ~US$1,200–1,600: HCMC/Hanoi centre, mixed diet, regular Grabs, insurance included — the standard nomad-professional reality. ~US$2,500: premium condo, Western-heavy lifestyle — Vietnam's ceiling is still below Bangkok's middle.
