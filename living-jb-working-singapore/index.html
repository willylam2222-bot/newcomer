The JB–Singapore commute: honest math on the border life

Earn in Singapore dollars, pay Malaysian rent: the arbitrage is real and thousands live it daily. The price is the world's busiest land border, twice a day.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 5 min read

Johor Bahru sits one kilometre from Singapore across the Causeway, with rents and living costs at a fraction of Singapore's. For Singapore earners, moving across the border can save serious money — the question is whether the commute costs you more in life than it saves in rent. The honest ledger:

The money side

  • Rent: a modern JB 2–3BR condo with pool runs roughly RM2,000–3,500 (~S$600–1,050) — versus S$3,000–4,500 for the Singapore equivalent. A landed house in JB costs less than a Singapore HDB flat's rent.
  • Living costs: groceries, dining and services run at a third to half of Singapore prices; the SGD-MYR exchange rate has trended in Singapore earners' favour for years.
  • The catch-lines: you'll likely want a car in JB (affordable there), Singapore CPF/housing benefits don't follow you across, and school choices get complicated for families.

The commute — the real price

  • The Causeway is the world's busiest land crossing (hundreds of thousands daily). Peak-hour crossings can run 1–2+ hours each way on bad days; off-peak motorbike commuters do it in 30–45 minutes. Many JB-based workers structure life around avoiding the peak entirely.
  • The game-changer: the RTS Link — the JB–Woodlands rapid transit line, targeted to open around end-2026/2027, designed to move 10,000 passengers/hour with co-located customs. When it opens, the crossing becomes a ~5-minute train ride plus clearance, and the whole JB-living equation improves dramatically. Property markets on both sides have been pricing this in for years.
Who it actually suits

Works well: Malaysians working in Singapore (the classic case), Singapore-employed foreigners with flexible/hybrid schedules, and families prioritising space over convenience. Works badly: anyone with rigid 9am starts in the CBD and low tolerance for border variance. Hybrid workers crossing 2–3 times a week are the current sweet spot.

Practical notes

  • Your immigration status must support it: this is a daily international border crossing — Singapore work pass holders cross freely, but living outside Singapore can have pass/tax implications worth checking with your employer.
  • Motorbike is the local commuter's weapon (dedicated lanes); buses and the shuttle train (Shuttle Tebrau) are the car-free options until RTS opens.
  • Try before committing: JB serviced apartments rent monthly — test-drive the commute for a month against your real schedule.
