Public drinking in Singapore: the rules nobody explains

Beer at a bar at midnight: fine. The same beer on the beach at midnight: an offence. Here's the line, exactly.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 4 min read

Since 2015, Singapore's Liquor Control Act has drawn a clean line around when and where you can drink outside licensed venues. Tourists trip over it constantly because nothing looks different at 10:30pm — but the law changes.

The core rules

  • No drinking in public places from 10:30pm to 7am. Parks, beaches, void decks, sidewalks, the Merlion steps — all covered. First offence can be fined up to S$1,000.
  • Retail sales stop at 10:30pm. Supermarkets and convenience stores can't sell takeaway alcohol after that — the shelves get roped off on the dot.
  • Licensed venues are unaffected. Bars, restaurants, hawker centres with licensed drink stalls: drink as late as they're open and licensed.

Liquor Control Zones: stricter

Geylang and Little India are designated Liquor Control Zones with extended no-drinking hours on weekends and public holidays, and higher penalties. If you're staying in either area, assume public drinking is off the table entirely and keep it to licensed venues.

The practical version

Buy your drinks before 10:30pm. After that, drink them at home, in your hotel, or at a licensed venue — never on the street, beach or park. That one habit keeps you entirely clear of the Act.

Related notes

  • Drinking at home or in your hotel room is always fine at any hour.
  • Alcohol is heavily taxed — bar prices will feel high (see our cost-of-living guide). Duty-free allowances at Changi are worth using on arrival.
  • Public drunkenness that causes annoyance is its own offence, ban hours or not.
