The work prompts worth stealing — with the method behind them

Most 'best prompts' lists are 100 variations of 'summarise this'. These ten earn a permanent spot in real workflows — and each teaches a reusable move.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 5 min read

A good work prompt has a job, your context, and constraints. These ten are battle-tested shapes — paste, fill the brackets, adapt. They work in any frontier assistant (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini).

Communication

  • The dreaded reply: "Draft a reply to this email [paste]. What I actually want to say: [blunt version]. Make it professional but unmistakably firm. Give me two versions: warm and cool." The move: separating what you mean from how it should sound.
  • The status update: "Turn these raw notes into a weekly update: done / in progress / blocked / needs decision. Executive tone, zero filler. [paste]" The move: forcing a format kills rambling.
  • The difficult message rehearsal: "I need to tell [person] that [hard thing]. Draft it, then play them and give me their three most likely reactions, then suggest how I handle each."

Meetings & documents

  • Notes → actions: "From this transcript [paste]: decisions made, action items with owners, open questions. Table."
  • The pre-meeting brief: "I'm meeting [who] about [what]. Background: [paste]. Give me 5 likely talking points, 3 questions I should ask, 1 risk to watch."
  • The document distiller: "Summarise this in 10 bullets, then separately list anything unusual, risky or missing. [paste]" The move: 'what's missing' is where AI earns its keep — anyone can summarise.

Thinking

  • The critic: "Here's my [plan/draft]. Attack it: what's weak, what would a skeptical [audience] push back on? Don't be polite. Then the single highest-impact fix." The most underused prompt in existence.
  • The decision doc: "Help me decide [X vs Y]. Ask me the 5 most important questions one at a time, then recommend with a confidence level and what would change your mind."
  • The presentation skeleton: "10-slide outline arguing [conclusion] to [audience]: headline + 3 points per slide, plus dedicated slides for the 2 hardest objections."
  • The template maker: after any task that went well — "Turn what we just did into a reusable prompt template for next time." This is how a prompt library compounds.
The one-line upgrade for every prompt above

Add your actual context: who you are, who it's for, what good looks like, one example of your voice. Generic in, generic out — the brackets are where the magic lives, not the template.
