Stop competing with AI. Start standing on it.

The people who lose to AI are the ones who do the exact task it does. The people who win use it to do the things their job actually rewards — faster, clearer, and more often than anyone else.

You don't need to become a "prompt engineer." You need to point AI at the boring, slow, or intimidating parts of your real job so you show up as the person who gets more done and is always the most prepared. Here are three of the ten to start with today:

1 · Turn your most tedious task into a repeatable workflow

The fastest way to free up hours: hand the boring, repeating part to a process you can reuse every week.

Copy · paste · fill the blanksHere's a task I do repeatedly at work: [describe it step by step]. Act as an operations expert. Turn this into a clear, repeatable workflow — number the steps, flag where mistakes usually happen, and give me a reusable template for the part AI can help with.

4 · Walk into any meeting as the most prepared person

Preparation reads as competence. This gets you there in 5 minutes.

Copy · paste · fill the blanksI have a meeting about: [topic/goal]. Attendees: [roles]. Give me: the 3 questions likely to come up, a strong answer to each, one smart question I should ask, and the single outcome I should push for.

10 · Make your work visible (without bragging)

Great work nobody sees doesn't count at review time. This turns your week into a clean record.

Copy · paste · fill the blanksHere's what I did this week: [dump everything, messy is fine]. Turn it into a 4-bullet "wins" summary framed around impact (what changed / what it enabled / numbers if any), for my manager or my review. Confident, not boastful.

Those are 3 of the 10.
