Making AI writing sound human: the actual techniques

AI writing has a smell: smooth, hedged, over-structured, allergic to a strong opinion. Removing that smell is a learnable skill — here's the method.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 5 min read

Readers increasingly recognise AI prose, and it costs you trust. The good news: the "AI voice" comes from predictable habits you can prompt and edit away. Here's what gives it away, and how to fix each.

The tells (and their antidotes)

The AI tellThe fix
Endless hedging ("it's important to note", "can vary")Ask for opinions and commitment: "take a clear position; cut every hedge"
Over-structure: every answer a listicle with headers"Write this as flowing prose, no bullet points, like a person talking"
Inflated vocabulary ("utilise", "leverage", "delve", "tapestry")"Use plain words. Short sentences. No corporate vocabulary."
Symmetry: everything in tidy threes, balanced both-sidesAsk for a real angle: "you're allowed to think one option is clearly better"
The wrap-up paragraph that restates everything"End on the strongest point, not a summary"
Em-dash overload and "not just X, but Y" constructionsEdit them out by hand — they're AI fingerprints in 2026

The single most powerful technique: feed it your voice

AI defaults to the average of all writing. Give it a target and it transforms:

Here are 2–3 paragraphs I wrote [paste]. Study my voice — sentence length, rhythm, how formal, what I do and don't do. Now write [new thing] in that exact voice. Match my quirks, don't smooth them out.

This one move does more than every "sound human" trick combined. Your writing has fingerprints; hand them to the model.

The edit pass that seals it

  • Read it aloud. Anywhere you stumble or wouldn't actually say it that way — rewrite. The ear catches AI-ness the eye misses.
  • Add one specific, concrete detail the AI couldn't know — a real example, a number, a named thing. Specificity is the opposite of AI vagueness.
  • Cut the first sentence. AI loves a throat-clearing opener; the real start is usually sentence two.
  • Break one rule. A fragment. A sentence that runs long because the thought did. Deliberate imperfection reads as human.
The honest boundary

These techniques make AI a better drafting and editing partner — not a way to pass off machine writing as your original thought where that matters (school, journalism, anything that trades on authenticity). The aim is writing that's genuinely yours, produced faster — not deception.
