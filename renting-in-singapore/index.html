Renting in Singapore: how it really works

Renting in Singapore is straightforward once you know the money mechanics — and expensive to get wrong if you don't.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 6 min read

Finding a place in Singapore is competitive but orderly. The mistakes that cost newcomers money aren't about taste — they're about not knowing how deposits, clauses and agent fees work before signing. Here's the essentials.

The upfront cash

ItemTypical norm
Security deposit1 month per year of lease (2 months for a 2-year lease)
Advance rent1 month, on signing (this is your first month)
Lease stamp duty~0.4% of total rent over the lease term (tenant usually pays)
Agent feeOften nothing for you on 1-year+ leases; a half-month may apply on lower-rent rooms

Rule of thumb: budget two months' rent upfront (deposit + advance) plus a small stamp duty before you get keys.

Two clauses that matter

  • Diplomatic clause — on a 2-year lease, this lets you break the lease after the first 12 months with about two months' notice if you must leave the country. If your plans could change, insist on it.
  • Minor repairs clause — caps what you pay per repair (commonly the first S$150–200), with the landlord covering the rest. Without it, a broken aircon compressor becomes your bill.

Whole unit vs a room

A common room in a shared flat can cost half or less of a whole unit. If you're solo or a student, this is the fastest way to cut your housing bill — you're mainly paying for privacy, not space.

Avoiding rental scams

The one rule

Never pay a deposit before viewing the actual unit. A "landlord" who is overseas, can't meet, and wants a transfer to "reserve" the place is the classic scam. Verify the agent's CEA registration number (every licensed agent has one) and see proof they own or represent the unit.

Timing

Demand and prices spike around the mid-year university intake and peak expat-move months (roughly June–August). If your dates are flexible, hunting in quieter months can lower what you pay for the same place. Signing a 2-year lease (with a diplomatic clause) also often unlocks a lower monthly rate.

Bottom line

Know the deposit maths, insist on the diplomatic and repairs clauses, view before you pay, and time your search — and renting here is smooth. Get one of those wrong and it's an expensive lesson.
