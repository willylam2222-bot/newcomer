Getting around Dubai without losing your mind

Dubai was built for cars, retrofitted with a great metro, and glued together by cheap taxis. The right mix depends on where you live and how much you hate walking in 40°C.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 4 min read

Unlike Singapore or Tokyo, Dubai isn't a city where transit covers everything — it's a long, linear city where the metro spine is excellent and everything off-spine is a ride-hail. Here's the practical system.

The Metro (and the Nol card)

  • Two main lines hug the city's spine (airport → Downtown → Marina). Clean, driverless, cheap — trips cost a few dirhams with a Nol card (buy at any station; also taps buses, trams and water buses).
  • Cabin rules matter: there's a Gold class (premium fare) and a women & children cabin — men entering it get fined. Eating and drinking anywhere in the system: fined.
  • Brutal truth: stations can be far from your actual destination, and a "10-minute walk" in July is a sweat event. The metro + short taxi combo is the local move.

Taxis & Careem

Street taxis (cream RTA cars) are metered, honest and plentiful; Careem (the local Uber, which also operates Uber here) is the app default. Rides are affordable by Western standards, and for two+ people often beat the metro on time for a small premium. Late night, it's the only real option off-spine.

Do you need a car?

  • Tourists: no. Metro + Careem covers everything.
  • Residents: probably eventually. Petrol is cheap, cars are affordable, and much of daily life (schools, big groceries, villa communities) assumes driving. Many expats hold out 6–12 months on Careem, then buy. Note: zero-tolerance drink-driving — any alcohol means Careem, no exceptions.
Renting-a-car footnote

Your home licence works for tourists from many countries (with an IDP for some); residents must convert to a UAE licence once on a residence visa. Salik (toll) gates bill automatically on rentals — check how your rental company charges them.
