Which Singapore work pass do you actually qualify for?

Singapore's work-pass system looks complicated but reduces to one question: what will you be paid, and does your profile score enough points?

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 6 min read

Every foreigner working in Singapore needs a pass, and which one you get shapes everything — salary floor, dependants, job mobility. Corporate-services sites bury this in upsell; here's the plain version.

The three main passes

PassWho it's for2026 salary floor (indicative)
Employment Pass (EP)Professionals, managers, executivesFrom ~S$5,600/month (~S$6,200 in financial services); rises with age — mid-40s applicants need roughly double
S PassMid-skilled technical staffLower floor (roughly S$3,300+, higher in finance); employer quota and levy apply
Work PermitSector workers (construction, marine, manufacturing, services, domestic)No universal floor; heavily regulated by sector and source country

COMPASS: the points system behind the EP

Since 2023, EP applications are scored under COMPASS — points for salary vs local benchmarks, qualifications, workforce diversity, and support for local employment, with bonuses for shortage occupations. You need 40 points to pass. Practical translation: a strong salary at a firm that isn't over-concentrated in one nationality usually clears it; borderline salaries at concentrated firms don't.

What each pass means for family

  • EP holders earning above a threshold (~S$6,000) can bring spouse and children on Dependant's Passes.
  • S Pass family thresholds are higher relative to salary, so many S Pass holders can't sponsor dependants.
  • Work Permit holders generally cannot bring dependants.
The honest read

Your employer drives this process — they apply, they hold the pass. Your job is to sanity-check the offer: is the salary above the floor for your age, and comfortably above it if you want dependants? Use MOM's official Self-Assessment Tool before you sign anything; it's free and gives a realistic verdict in minutes.

Timeline & practical notes

  • EP processing is typically days to a few weeks; you'll get an In-Principle Approval (IPA) letter first — that's the document banks and landlords will accept early on.
  • Passes are tied to the employer; changing jobs means a fresh application.
  • Figures above are indicative as of mid-2026 — MOM adjusts floors periodically, so verify current numbers on mom.gov.sg before planning.
