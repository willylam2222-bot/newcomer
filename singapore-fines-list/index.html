Every fine a visitor can realistically get in Singapore

Everyone knows Singapore is 'a fine city.' Almost nobody online keeps the list current or tells you what's actually enforced. Here's both.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 5 min read

Fines are how Singapore keeps public spaces clean, quiet and orderly. Most visitors never pay one — because the rules are easy to follow once you know them. This is the one-page version, with a note on how strictly each is enforced in practice.

The everyday fines

OffenceTypical penaltyEnforcement reality
Littering (incl. cigarette butts, spat-out gum)Commonly ~S$300 for a first offence; repeat offenders face court and Corrective Work Orders (cleaning in public)Actively enforced
Eating or drinking on the MRT/LRT (including water)Up to S$500Real; signage everywhere
Smoking outside permitted areasComposition fines in the hundreds; up to S$1,000 in courtActively enforced; look for yellow-box smoking areas
Jaywalking (crossing within 50m of a crossing, or against the light)Fixed penalties from ~S$50; higher if it goes to courtEnforced in waves; don't test it
Not returning your hawker trayWarning first; fines for repeat casesEnforced since 2021 — just return the tray
Drinking alcohol in public 10:30pm–7amUp to S$1,000 first offenceReal, stricter in Liquor Control Zones
Not flushing a public toiletAn actual offence with fines on the booksFamous, rarely enforced
Feeding pigeons/monkeysFines up to four figuresEnforced, especially wildlife
Using someone's Wi-Fi without permissionComputer Misuse Act offenceRare, but real
The two that aren't "fines"

Vaping (penalties sharply increased in 2026 — see our 2026 vaping law guide) and anything drug-related (criminal, potentially capital — see our drug-law guide). Treat both as absolute no-go, not "risk a fine."

How composition fines work

Most minor offences are settled by "composition" — you pay a fixed amount instead of going to court. Amounts step up for repeat offences. Figures above are indicative as of mid-2026; agencies adjust them, so treat this as the map, not the statute.

The honest summary

Don't litter, keep food off the trains, smoke only in marked boxes, cross at crossings, return your tray, and keep public drinking to daytime and licensed venues. Do that and the "fine city" costs you nothing.
