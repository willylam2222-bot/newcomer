Taking medication to Japan: stricter than you think

Japan is relaxed about many things. Medication is not one of them — and the strictest rule catches exactly the travellers who least expect it: ADHD patients and people carrying cold medicine.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 5 min read

Japan's pharmaceutical rules are among the strictest of any major destination, and they differ from the usual pattern — some things fine elsewhere are banned here, while a few surprises run the other way. Here's the practical map.

The absolute ban that shocks people: amphetamine ADHD meds

Adderall cannot enter Japan. At all.

Amphetamine-based medication (Adderall, Dexedrine, Evekeo) is classified as a prohibited stimulant under Japanese law. No prescription, doctor's letter or permit makes it legal — travellers have been detained over it. If you take Adderall, talk to your doctor about a temporary switch: lisdexamfetamine (Vyvanse) and methylphenidate (Ritalin/Concerta) can be brought with an advance permit, in limited quantities.

What needs paperwork: the Yakkan Shoumei

Japan's import certificate (Yakkan Shoumei, now applied for via the online "Import Confirmation" system) is required when you exceed the personal-use thresholds:

  • More than 1 month's supply of prescription medicines
  • More than 2 months' supply of most OTC medicines
  • Any quantity of certain controlled psychotropics (many sleep/anxiety meds have specific limits) and injectables (except insulin with prescription up to a month)

Apply 2+ weeks ahead with your prescription and itinerary; carry the certificate with the meds in original packaging.

The cold-medicine surprise

Some Western cold and allergy products are restricted because of their stimulant-adjacent ingredients — notably high-dose pseudoephedrine (some Sudafed formulations) and codeine-containing products above small thresholds. Small personal quantities of ordinary combination cold meds are generally tolerated, but the clean move is simple: buy cold medicine in Japan — pharmacies (Matsumoto Kiyoshi and friends) are excellent and everywhere.

What's easier than you'd expect

  • Ordinary prescriptions under a month's supply: no paperwork, just original packaging + prescription copy.
  • Insulin and EpiPens: fine with prescription (up to a month without permit).
  • CBD is legal in Japan if THC-free — a sharp contrast with Singapore and the UAE. (THC remains strictly illegal; imported CBD products must meet Japan's zero-THC standard, so buying locally is safer than carrying.)

Pre-flight checklist

  • ADHD on amphetamines → doctor conversation now, not at the airport.
  • Any psychotropic/sleep/anxiety med or >1 month supply → check MHLW thresholds → apply for the import confirmation 2+ weeks out.
  • Everything in original packaging, prescriptions in hand luggage.
  • Cold/allergy meds → just buy them there.
