MM2H: what Malaysia's long-stay visa looks like now

MM2H went from famously accessible, to briefly impossible, to a tiered compromise. If your research shows three different rule sets — that's why. Here's the current shape.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 5 min read

Malaysia My Second Home (MM2H) is the country's flagship long-stay route: a renewable multi-year residence visa for foreigners who park money in Malaysia. Its rules have swung hard — loose for years, tightened sharply in 2021, then rebuilt in 2024 into a tiered system. Because exact figures have moved repeatedly, treat the numbers below as the framework and verify current terms on the official portal before planning.

  • Deposits sit in a Malaysian bank; partial withdrawals are allowed for approved purposes (property, healthcare, education) above a maintained floor.
  • The 2024 revision added property-purchase conditions (minimum values by tier/state) and lowered the minimum age (participation from 25+, with dependants allowed).
  • Special lower-threshold tracks exist for specific zones (e.g. borderless-economy initiatives in Sarawak and the JB special zone have run their own variants).

Who MM2H actually suits now

  • Retirees and semi-retirees with capital who want long, stable residence in a low-cost, high-infrastructure country — still the core case, especially at Silver.
  • Families positioning for education — international schools at a third of Singapore prices, with the visa solving the school-enrolment residency question.
  • Not remote workers — MM2H doesn't permit local employment and the capital lockup makes no sense for nomads (Malaysia's DE Rantau nomad pass is that lane instead).
The honest calculus

MM2H's value is certainty: decade-plus residence in a country where your money goes far, without visa-run anxiety. Its cost is opportunity cost on the deposit plus property conditions. If the Silver deposit is a stretch, you're likely better on renewable long-stay alternatives; if it's comfortable, MM2H remains one of Asia's best residence-for-capital deals — quietly better than most 'golden visa' equivalents.

Applying

Applications run through licensed MM2H agents (mandatory under current rules) with health-insurance and background-check requirements. Timelines run months, not weeks. The official portal (mm2h.gov.my) lists current terms and licensed agents — start there, not with a marketing site's version of the rules.
