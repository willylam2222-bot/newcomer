Getting to the World Cup final at MetLife: the last-minute playbook

The final is at MetLife Stadium, New Jersey, on July 19 — and yes, you can still organise the trip. Here's the honest sequence, cost reality included.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 5 min read

Deciding late to fly for a World Cup final is expensive but completely doable — New York is one of the few metros that can absorb a final's crowd. The checklist, in dependency order:

1. Entry to the US (do this first, today)

  • Visa Waiver countries (most of Europe, Japan, Singapore, Australia etc.): apply for ESTA online — usually approved within minutes to 72 hours. Do it before booking anything.
  • Everyone else needs a B visa — appointment backlogs make this the trip-killer if you don't already hold one. Check your status honestly before spending money.
  • Flying via Canada or Mexico legs? Each has its own entry requirement (eTA / FMM) — don't improvise a multi-country itinerary without checking each.

2. Where to stay (and where not to bother)

  • Stay in Manhattan or along the NJ Transit/PATH corridors (Secaucus, Hoboken, Jersey City) — the stadium sits in a parking ocean in East Rutherford; there's no reason to sleep near it.
  • Final-week hotel prices surge but NYC's supply is vast — mid-town business hotels and airport-adjacent options soften the worst of it. Book refundable, then optimise.

3. Match day at MetLife: what veterans know

  • Train, not car. NJ Transit runs event service from Secaucus Junction to Meadowlands station at the stadium — from Manhattan, it's Penn Station → Secaucus → shuttle train. Allow 90+ minutes door-to-gate on final day and expect crush loads both ways.
  • Ride-hailing to the stadium hits event traffic and drop-off perimeters — it's the slow, pricey option, not the premium one.
  • Clear-bag policy and airport-style security: small clear bags only, arrive hours early, hydrate — a July final in New Jersey means full summer heat in largely shade-free surroundings.
  • The stadium is cashless; tickets live in the official app with your phone charged — bring a power bank (in your clear bag).
The honest budget for a late decision

Long-haul flights booked days out: expect premium pricing. Add final-week hotels, and the ticket itself (see our ticket-buying guide) — a realistic late-decision total runs well into four figures. The cheaper version of the same memory: semifinals, the third-place match, or the final at a host-city fan festival with 20,000 friends.
