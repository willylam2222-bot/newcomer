What Dubai actually costs to live in

No income tax pulls people in; rent and school fees decide whether the math actually works. Here's the honest ledger.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 5 min read

Dubai's pitch is simple: tax-free salary, sunshine, safety. The reality check is that housing and (for families) schooling absorb much of the tax saving. Whether Dubai is "cheap" depends almost entirely on those two lines.

Rent — the dominant line (indicative yearly, paid in cheques)

Quirks that surprise newcomers: rent is quoted yearly and traditionally paid in 1–4 post-dated cheques (more cheques = slightly higher price); add a 5% deposit, ~5% agency fee, and DEWA (utilities) deposits upfront. Budget real cash to move in.

The tax math, honestly

  • No income tax on salaries — the headline is real.
  • But: 5% VAT on most spending, housing fee (~5% of rent, billed via DEWA), and for families, school fees are the hidden tax — decent international schools run AED 30–100k+ per child per year. A tax-free package that ignores school fees isn't a real number.

Everyday costs

  • Cheap: petrol, taxis/Careem (relative to income), delivery food, domestic help, gyms in towers, phone plans (mid).
  • Moderate: groceries (imported goods add up), utilities (aircon-heavy summers spike DEWA bills).
  • Expensive: alcohol (licensed venue prices), brunches and beach clubs, anything "lifestyle" — Dubai monetises its own image efficiently.
The one-line verdict

Single professional in JVC on a decent package: Dubai is comfortably affordable and the tax saving is real. Family of four wanting Marina + international school: run the numbers twice — school fees plus rent can quietly out-eat the tax advantage.
