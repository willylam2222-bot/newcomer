What to wear in Dubai — and where the PDA line really is

Dubai's dress rules are simpler than the forums suggest: dress for the venue, and keep affection low-key in public. That's 95% of it.

By the Newcomer editorial team · Last verified July 2026 · 4 min read

Nobody is measuring hemlines in Dubai. The city hosts beach clubs and designer nightlife alongside mosques and family malls, and the code switches by venue — exactly like it does at home, just with the modesty dial one notch higher in shared public spaces.

Dress, by place

PlaceThe norm
Malls, souks, restaurantsCasual with shoulders/knees loosely in mind — shorts and sundresses are everywhere; ultra-revealing draws looks and occasionally a polite reminder sign
Beaches, pools, beach clubsRegular swimwear is completely fine (bikinis included); topless is not. Cover up when you leave the sand
Bars, clubs, brunchDress up — Dubai nightlife is dressy; door policies care more about style than modesty
Mosques (visiting)Covered arms/legs; women cover hair — abayas/scarves are lent at major mosques
Government buildings, courtsConservative — covered shoulders and knees, enforced
Old Dubai / Deira / local neighbourhoodsDial modesty up a notch out of courtesy

PDA: where the line actually is

  • Fine: holding hands, an arm around a shoulder, a greeting peck between partners in tourist settings.
  • Draws complaints: kissing in public, cuddling on beaches or in malls — the offence mechanism is usually a complaint from an offended member of the public, so family-heavy spaces are the sensitive ones.
  • Genuinely risky: anything sexual in public, drunk affection spilling into streets or taxis — this is where "PDA" cases actually come from, usually with alcohol involved.
What changed in recent years

The 2020–21 reforms decriminalised unmarried cohabitation — couples share hotel rooms freely and nobody asks. The old horror stories mostly predate this. What hasn't changed: public decency law still responds to complaints, so discretion in shared spaces remains the rule.

Ramadan adjustment

During Ramadan, cover shoulders/knees in public spaces as a courtesy, keep PDA to effectively zero, and follow venue leads — the city visibly shifts registers for the month and visitors are expected to meet it halfway.
